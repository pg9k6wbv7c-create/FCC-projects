Tiger Woods

The man who captivated an entire generation of people who never considered swinging a golf club

Highlights of Tiger's Career

Tiger Woods had an illustrious career spanning over two decades, marked by numerous achievements and milestones in professional golf. We can break his career down into three distinct phases:

Rise to Prominence (1996-2008)

  • 1997 - Wins his first Masters by 12 strokes. Becomes the youngest person to win The Masters at age 21. Reaches World No. 1.
  • 2000 - Wins US Open by record 15 strokes. Becomes the youngest person to win all 4 majors.
  • 2001 - Wins Masters and holds all 4 professional major titles simultaneously.
  • 2008 - Wins US Open with a fractured leg. Later reveals torn Achilles tendon.

Injury and Scandal (2009-2013)

  • 2009 - Crashes car. Scandal surfaces about infedelity and prescription drug abuse.
  • 2010 - Returns, but plagued with injury.
  • 2013 - Returns to true form, winning multiple Tour events.
  • 2014 - Several back injuries lead to multiple surgeries.

Remarkable Comeback (2018-Present)

  • 2019 - Wins Masters with a historic comeback that really shocked the world.
  • 2021 - Major car crash leaves Tiger injured yet again, with no return likely.
